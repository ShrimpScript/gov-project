## Constitution Limits Power

The Constitution both grants and restricts power, which is essential for a stable government and not an all powerful one. 
The Founders didn't trust future people to not abuse their power, so they created a system where power is separated and distributed, 
making it very difficult for one person to take over.


## Separation of Powers

The powers in the federal government are separated into three distinct branches which all have different jobs. This prevents any single group from having total control or total power,
mainly to decentralize power.

*   **Article I (Legislative):** Congress makes the law.
*   **Article II (Executive):** The President enforces the law.
*   **Article III (Judicial):** The Supreme Court interprets the law.

## Checks and Balances

Seperating powers is still not enough though, you have to make sure the branches can fight back against each other in case one tries to take over. Each branch has a way to check the others, examples being:
impeachment, veto, judicial review, etc.

## The Bill of Rights

The first ten amendments is a list of rights that are directed at the government of what it cannnot strictly do. It cannot prohibit speech, establish religion, or conduct unreasonable searches. These are hard limits that protect individual liberty from the majority, again, limiting the power of the federal government.

## Summary

In summary, the Consitution limits powers of the federal government by establishing a system of checks and balances, seperation of powers, and a Bill of Rights that strictly forbids the federal government from doing certain things, and therefore limits powers of the federal government.
