## Centralized Power

The main concerns in the constitution were that there would be a centralized power in the government, which contradicted their independence from Great Britain.

## The Missing Bill of Rights

The constitution also had no Bill of Rights which was a major concern because the Anti-Federalists believed that if the rights of the people were never clearly listed in writing, the government would eventually go against them.

## Presidential Power

Another major concern was that the President held too much power in the military and foreign policy that they would essentially be another king.

## The Great Debate

This was also a very big, national argument between two groups of people: Anti-Federalists (believed in the government being limited) and the Federalists (believed in a stronger federal government). These concerns were mainly reflected by the Anti-Federalists, which eventually led to the creation of the Bill of Rights that we have today.
